Pele

Was a Brazilian professional footballer who played as a forward.

This is the life of Pele

  • 1940 - Born Edson Arantes do Nascimento on October 23 in Três Corações, Minas Gerais, Brazil, to a poor family. Nicknamed "Dico" early on, he later earns the nickname "Pelé."
  • 1956 - Joins Santos FC at age 15 after being scouted by Waldemar de Brito. Begins playing professionally, quickly gaining attention for his extraordinary talent.
  • 1957 - Makes his debut for the Brazilian national team at age 16, scoring in a match against Argentina. Becomes the youngest player to debut for Brazil.
  • 1958 - Wins his first World Cup with Brazil at age 17 in Sweden, scoring six goals, including two in the final against Sweden. Becomes a global sensation.
  • 1961 - Declared a "national treasure" by the Brazilian government, preventing him from being transferred to foreign clubs.
  • 1962 - Wins his second World Cup with Brazil in Chile, though an injury limits his participation. Brazil successfully defends their title.
  • 1966 - Plays in the World Cup in England, but Brazil is eliminated early. Pelé faces harsh tackling, raising concerns about his safety on the pitch.
  • 1969 - Scores his 1,000th career goal on November 19 at the Maracanã Stadium, a penalty kick for Santos against Vasco da Gama, a milestone celebrated worldwide.
  • 1970 - Wins his third World Cup with Brazil in Mexico, scoring in the final against Italy. His performance cements his legacy as the world’s greatest footballer.
  • 1971 - Retires from the Brazilian national team after 92 appearances and 77 goals. His final match is a friendly against Yugoslavia.
  • 1974 - Announces retirement from Santos FC but is later convinced to return to football.
  • 1975 - Signs with the New York Cosmos in the North American Soccer League, bringing global attention to soccer in the United States.
  • 1977 - Retires from professional football after a farewell match between the Cosmos and Santos. Scores his final goal in the exhibition game.
  • 1994 - Named UNESCO Goodwill Ambassador, beginning decades of humanitarian work promoting peace and education.
  • 1999 - Named Athlete of the Century by the International Olympic Committee, despite never competing in the Olympics.
  • 2014 - Publishes his autobiography, Pelé: Why Soccer Matters, reflecting on his life and the global impact of football.
  • 2022 - Dies on December 29 at age 82 in São Paulo, Brazil, after a battle with colon cancer. His passing prompts global tributes to his unparalleled legacy.
